English language requirements for postgraduate applicants

Depending on your nationality and where you have studied previously, we may ask you to provide a recognised English language qualification.

The English language requirements vary depending on the course that you are applying for.

On this page you will find a list of English language requirements by department and these are listed under the faculty that they are part of.

Ways to meet our English language requirements

Entry in 2023

There are several ways that you can provide evidence of your English language ability, details are listed below.

English Language Tests Accordion

  • Recognised English language qualifications

    We accept the following tests as evidence of English language ability:

    • IELTS Academic (UKVI Approved)
    • IELTS Academic (Other Centres)
    • IELTS Online
    • TOEFL IBT (including My Best Scores)
    • TOEFL IBT Home Edition
    • Pearson PTE Academic
    • Pearson PTE UKVI
    • Pearson PTE Academic Online
    • LanguageCert International ESOL
    • LanguageCert International ESOL SELT

    Our required scores vary by course and you can find the scores needed for each department on this page.

    IELTS, TOEFL, Pearson and LanguageCert certificates are valid for 2 years from the test date and your certificate will need to be valid on the start date of your studies at Lancaster. If you have taken a test multiple times, we cannot combine scores together to meet the requirements.

    If you take the Pearson test, you will need to give permission for Lancaster University to verify your score.

    If your English language score does not meet our requirements, we may be able to consider you for one of our pre-sessional English language programmes.

    The following English language qualifications may also be accepted.

    • Cambridge English: Advanced CAE
    • Cambridge English: Proficiency (CPE)
    • CaMLA ECPE
    • TRINITY ISE

    If you have one of these qualifications, please contact us for the requirements.

  • High school English

    If you have not taken one of our recognised English language qualifications we may be able to accept an alternative English language qualification. The alternatives we may be able to consider are detailed below by country.

    Botswana Cambridge Overseas School Certificate (English) Grades 1-6 inclusive / Botswana General Certificate of Secondary Education (BGCSE) in English at Grade C or above
    Brunei Cambridge GCE O Level (English) Grade 6 or above
    Cameroon Cameroon GCE O Level (English) Grade C or above
    Ghana GCE O Level (English) Grade C or above / Senior Secondary Certificate Examination (SSCE) in English, Grade C6 or above
    India Indian Standard XII English at 80% or above
    Kenya Kenyan Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) in English at Grade C or above
    Malaysia Malaysian 1119 syllabus (UCLES) English at Grade C or above / GCE O Level (English) Grade C or above
    Mauritius GCE O Level / GCSE English, Grade C or above
    Nigeria WAEC/WASSCE/NECO in English at Grade C6 or above
    Philippines High School Diploma in English at Grade C or above
    Singapore Singapore-Cambridge GCE O Level (English) at Grade C6 or above
    South Africa Senior Certificate (Matric) in English at 60% (Grade C) or above / Standard 10 Certificate (English)
    Tanzania Tanzanian Certificate of Secondary Education (TCSE) in English / Advanced Certificate of Secondary Education (ACSE) in English at Grade C or above
    Zambia Zambian Secondary School Certificate (ZSSC) in English at Grade C or above
    Zimbabwe GCE O Level (English), Grade C or above

    If you are applying for a Postgraduate course and your country is not listed above and if you have you taken one of the recognised English Language qualifications, please contact the Admissions Team.

    If your High School English Language qualification was awarded more than 10 years ago you may also be asked to take part in an English Language interview or provide a standard English Language test.

  • Previous UK study

    We may be able to consider waiving our English language requirements if you have:

    • Studied in the UK or other majority English-speaking country for at least 2 years of your undergraduate degree and/or for at least 1 year if you studied a postgraduate degree; and
    • Completed and been awarded your degree no more than three years before the intended start of your chosen course
  • Majority English speaking countries

    The following countries are those deemed to be a majority English-speaking country:

    • Antigua and Barbuda
    • Australia
    • The Bahamas
    • Barbados
    • Belize
    • Dominica
    • Grenada
    • Guyana
    • Ireland
    • Jamaica
    • Malta
    • New Zealand
    • St Kitts and Nevis
    • St Lucia
    • St Vincent and the Grenadines
    • Trinidad and Tobago
    • UK
    • USA

    If you are a national of one of the above countries, you are not required to submit evidence of your English language ability.

    If you are not a national of one of the above countries but have completed a full degree in one of the above countries, you will not be required to submit further evidence of your English Language if you have:

    • Completed and been awarded your degree no more than three years before the intended start of your chosen course.
